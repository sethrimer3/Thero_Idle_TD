# Character Sheet  
## Professors of Project Thero

The six members of **Project Thero** each represent a different domain of cognition.  
Inside the mindscape, their disciplines manifest as **spires**, conceptual structures shaping the architecture of thought itself.

---

## Slade Mercer — Protagonist  
**Discipline:** Mathematics  

Slade is the primary test subject of Project Thero. His consciousness was mapped over a year and a half using the **Mind Geiger (MG)**, nicknamed the **Mind Gate**.

He believes mathematics may be **self-existent**, embedded in the structure of reality.

Inside the mindscape, mathematical structures become architecture. Many enemies manifest as corrupted equations, recursive paradoxes, or unstable proofs.

Unknown to those who stole the technology, Slade's cognitive architecture contains the **Master Key** to the system.

**Signature line:**  
*"If the structure exists, the proof exists somewhere inside it."*

---

## Tess “Tessa” Albright  
**Discipline:** Psychology / Linguistics  

Research Areas:
- Relational Frame Theory  
- Generative Linguistics (Chomsky)  
- Hermeneutics  

Tess is Slade’s closest intellectual rival and friend. For years they debated one central question:

> Are mathematical structures real, or are they constructs of the human mind?

Inside the mindscape she manipulates symbolic meaning and conceptual relationships.

She eventually enters the mindscape to join Slade.

**Signature line:**  
*"Meaning isn't stored in symbols. It's created between them."*

---

## Matt Kline  
**Discipline:** Engineering / Systems Architecture  

Matt built the physical hardware of the **Mind Geiger (MG)** and the **Mind Gate** interface.

His systems allow consciousness to map into the cognitive realm.

Inside the mindscape his influence manifests through structural stability and engineered constructs.

**Signature line:**  
*"Start with first principles. If the axiom holds, the structure will hold."*

---

## Isaac Rao  
**Discipline:** Neuroscience / Physics  

Isaac developed the model describing the **wavelength of consciousness**, allowing the Mind Gate to synchronize human cognition with the interface.

He views physics and consciousness as deeply connected systems.

**Signature line:**  
*"Every mind has a frequency. The trick is learning how to resonate without collapsing."*

---

## Lydia Moreno  
**Discipline:** Biochemistry  

Lydia designed the biological interface that allows the human brain to safely synchronize with the Mind Gate.

She focuses on maintaining neurological stability during mapping.

**Signature line:**  
*"Life maintains structure through balance. The mind is no different."*

---

## Dr. Florida Sato  
**Discipline:** Medicine  

Florida was assigned by the university as the project’s medical supervisor. Initially outside the research group, they gradually became part of the team.

Florida monitors Slade’s biological stability during the experiment.

**Signature line:**  
*"A mind can endure more than you think—if you stabilize the damage before it spreads."*

# Prologue

## Journal — Page 592

We finished the final entanglement requirements for the ℘ gate today. The Weierstrass fractal was the last key—its structure resonated perfectly with the measured frequency of consciousness.

It feels strange.

We’ve been working on this project for so long that reaching this point almost feels unreal. I find myself nostalgic for the earlier days—when we were younger, staying late into the night, slowly filling the lab fridge with caffeine and cheap food.

Those nights bonded us.

Lab C137 slowly stopped feeling like a workplace and started feeling like something closer to sacred ground.

Tomorrow we begin the final testing.

---

# Chapter 1 — Isolation

Slade awakens alone inside the mindscape after the Mind Gate is stolen during the experiment.

He experiments with the environment and realizes the structures respond to **mathematical logic and conceptual focus**.

Eventually he concludes the machine must have been **taken from the lab**, leaving him stranded inside the system.

---

# Chapter 2 — First Contact

After a long silence, faint signals appear in the mindscape.

The professors manage to establish unstable communication using salvaged equipment.

They reveal the truth:

The **funding group stole the Mind Gate during the experiment**.

The machine is still active somewhere in the world.

Slade cannot yet be extracted, but communication is restored.

---

# Chapter 3 — The Threshold

The professors stabilize the connection and begin helping Slade navigate the mindscape.

Isaac discovers a method to reverse the mapping process and return Slade’s consciousness to his body.

An extraction sequence begins.

But during the synchronization process, they detect something unexpected:

The stolen Mind Gate is **broadcasting cognitive signals globally**.

Someone is attempting to influence human cognition.

Slade is given the chance to leave.

He chooses to stay.

---

# Chapter 4 — The Fires

The professors realize the stolen machines are being used to influence the **collective unconscious**.

The broadcasts amplify archetypal patterns such as fear, tribalism, and hostility.

Inside the mindscape these influences appear as spreading distortions.

Slade begins stabilizing the structures to counteract the signals.

The team realizes the mindscape reflects the **shared cognitive layer of humanity**.

A global **cognitive war** has begun.

---

# Chapter 5 — The Hunt

Researchers using the stolen technology realize someone inside the system is disrupting their broadcasts.

They begin searching for the **source of the interference**.

A manhunt begins for the individual with the Master Key.

The professors become fugitives and hide Slade’s physical body in a remote cabin.

Before losing communication entirely, they make one final decision.

Tess volunteers to enter the mindscape.

She joins Slade.

---

# Chapter 6 — The Vigil

Together, Slade and Tess defend the mindscape.

Hostile agents begin entering the cognitive realm to remove them.

Without contact with the outside world, they continue stabilizing the structures of the collective unconscious.

Meanwhile the professors anonymously spread research explaining how people can recognize cognitive manipulation.

As awareness spreads, the hostile broadcasts lose power.

Slade and Tess remain inside the mindscape, maintaining the balance.

For now, the vigil continues.

---

# Epilogue (Summary)

Decades pass.

Slade and Tess continue shaping the collective unconscious toward cooperation, empathy, and awareness.

Humanity gradually becomes more resistant to manipulation.

Eventually the remaining professors rebuild the Mind Gate safely and retrieve them.

The world they return to is peaceful—but unfamiliar.

The quiet result of a war fought almost entirely inside the human mind.
